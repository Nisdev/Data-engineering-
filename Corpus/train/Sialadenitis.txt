Sialadenitis is a condition characterized by inflammation and enlargement of one or more of the salivary glands, the glands that secrete saliva into the mouth. There are both acute and chronic forms. Sialadenitis is often associated with pain, tenderness, redness, and gradual, localized swelling of the affected area. The exact cause of sialadenitis is not known. The exact cause of sialadenitis is unknown.  In some cases, the condition may be associated with the formation of salivary gland stones (sialolithiasis). Sialadenitis affects males and females in equal numbers.  It shows no racial biases. The disorder is often diagnosed by means of a thorough patient history and physical examination. Recent advances in endoscopic equipment make the diagnosis somewhat easier.
